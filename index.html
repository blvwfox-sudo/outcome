Protocol Overview

SSP-01 combines several peptide “modules.” Each module supports a specific outcome — such as growth hormone (GH) support, tissue repair, or fat metabolism. Your clinician may include some or all of these based on your goals, history, and lab work.

Module Peptides Primary Goals
GH / Performance CJC-1295 (no DAC) + Ipamorelin Support natural GH pulses, recovery, sleep, and body composition.
Healing & Recovery BPC-157 + TB-500 Soft-tissue recovery, tendon/ligament support, training resilience.
Collagen & Longevity GHK-Cu Support collagen, tissue quality, and long-term resilience.
Fat Loss (Optional) AOD-9604 Additional fat metabolism support during cutting/recomp phases.
Advanced Muscle (Optional) IGF-1 LR3 / IGF-1 DES Advanced, clinician-guided tools focused on muscle density and nutrient partitioning.

Peptide Breakdown — SSP-01

Below, each peptide is explained in plain language: what it’s for, when it’s typically used in the protocol, and why it’s part of the Super Soldier stack. All dosing and reconstitution content is educational only.

CJC-1295 (No DAC)
GH axis support
Core GH Stack

CJC-1295 (no DAC) is often discussed as a way to support the body’s own growth hormone (GH) pulses rather than replacing GH directly.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Typical dose range: 100–200 mcg per administration.
  • Common frequency: 1–2× per day (for example, evening ± optional morning).
  • Often taken about 1 hour before bed, away from food.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Supports natural GH release patterns.
  • Often used for recovery, body composition, and sleep quality support.
  • Works synergistically with Ipamorelin in many protocols.
Why It’s in SSP-01
  • Acts as the “signal” that primes the GH axis in a more physiologic way.
  • Created to form the base of the performance and recovery aspect of SSP-01.
  • Helps set the stage for better tissue repair, fat loss, and training adaptation.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

Clinically, CJC-1295 (no DAC) is supplied as a dry (lyophilized) powder. A licensed professional typically:

  • Adds a measured amount of sterile/bacteriostatic water to the vial.
  • Calculates the resulting concentration (for example, micrograms per milliliter).
  • Prescribes a specific dose and explains how much volume to draw for each injection.

This explanation is to help you understand the math and terminology you may hear from your clinician. It is not an instruction to mix or inject anything yourself.

Ipamorelin
GH secretagogue
Core GH Stack

Ipamorelin is another GH secretagogue that is often paired with CJC-1295 to amplify GH pulses while targeting a favorable side-effect profile.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Typical dose range: 100–200 mcg per administration.
  • Common frequency: 1–2× per day, frequently aligned with CJC-1295 timing.
  • Often part of a pre-bed protocol to pair with natural nighttime GH surges.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Supports GH release without large spikes in cortisol or prolactin.
  • Often used to support recovery, sleep quality, and body composition.
  • Pairs naturally with CJC-1295 in GH-support stacks.
Why It’s in SSP-01
  • Designed to amplify the GH signal started by CJC-1295.
  • Helps create a stronger, yet still physiologic, GH environment for recovery and performance.
  • Part of the “core” Super Soldier GH / performance stack.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

As with CJC-1295, Ipamorelin is typically supplied as a powder and is reconstituted by a clinician:

  • Sterile/bacteriostatic water is added under controlled conditions.
  • A concentration is calculated (for example, mcg per mL).
  • A clear dosing schedule and volume-per-injection are provided to the patient.

You should never attempt to mix or inject Ipamorelin without direct medical supervision.

BPC-157
Tissue repair support
Recovery Stack

BPC-157 is frequently discussed in the context of tendon, ligament, and soft-tissue support, as well as gut integrity.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Typical daily range: 250–500 mcg.
  • May be taken once per day or split (AM / PM) under a clinician’s guidance.
  • Sometimes targeted near an area of concern, based on medical direction.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Often discussed for tendon/ligament support.
  • Frequently mentioned regarding tissue recovery and inflammation modulation.
  • Popular in rehab or heavy training environments.
Why It’s in SSP-01
  • SSP-01 assumes intense physical effort and load.
  • BPC-157 is included to help support tissues under that stress.
  • Acts as a core part of SSP-01’s “Wolverine-style” recovery engine.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

BPC-157 is also typically reconstituted from powder form. In a medical setting:

  • A clinician adds sterile/bacteriostatic water to the vial.
  • The resulting concentration is documented in the patient instructions.
  • Volume drawn into a syringe corresponds to the prescribed microgram dose.

This overview is to help patients ask better questions and understand dosing math — not to enable unsupervised use.

TB-500
Systemic regeneration
Recovery Stack

TB-500 is often discussed for its systemic effects on tissue healing and mobility through actin-related pathways.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Typical weekly range: 2–5 mg per week.
  • Often split into 2–3 injections per week.
  • Used as part of a multi-week cycle under clinician supervision.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • System-wide support for tissue healing processes.
  • Often discussed for improved recovery and joint comfort.
  • Complements the more localized support of BPC-157.
Why It’s in SSP-01
  • Designed to support the entire musculoskeletal system, not just one area.
  • Pairs with heavy training, where multiple joints / tissues may be stressed at once.
  • Helps create the “Wolverine” feel inside a performance-focused program.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

TB-500 is typically supplied in higher milligram vials. A clinician:

  • Determines the appropriate total dose and cycle.
  • Reconstitutes the vial with sterile/bacteriostatic water.
  • Provides exact instructions on how much volume equals each weekly or split dose.

Always rely on explicit medical instructions for mixing volume and injection technique.

GHK-Cu
Collagen & tissue quality
Longevity Stack

GHK-Cu is a copper-bound peptide often associated with collagen formation, skin quality, and tissue remodeling.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Typical daily range: 1–2 mg.
  • Often taken in the evening, overlapping with recovery-focused signals.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Supports collagen production and tissue quality.
  • Frequently discussed for skin and hair appearance.
  • May support connective tissue resilience.
Why It’s in SSP-01
  • SSP-01 doesn’t just address performance; it also favors long-term tissue health.
  • GHK-Cu is included to support the “building materials” behind tendons, skin, and fascia.
  • Pairs well with a recovery-heavy program where tissues are repairing daily.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

In medical practice, GHK-Cu may be used in topical or injectable formats. When injectable:

  • A clinician reconstitutes the powder with sterile fluid.
  • Documents the concentration and daily dose clearly.
  • Explains whether the plan is more aesthetic, structural, or both.

The exact form, route, and dose should always be personally prescribed.

AOD-9604
Fat metabolism support
Optional Module

AOD-9604 is a peptide fragment often discussed in the context of fat metabolism and body recomposition strategies.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Typical daily value discussed: around 300 mcg.
  • Commonly mentioned timing: morning, fasted.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Often used as an adjunct to diet and activity for fat-loss support.
  • Discussed as more specific to metabolic pathways rather than whole-GH effects.
Why It’s in SSP-01
  • Provides additional metabolic leverage during cutting or recomposition phases.
  • Used when body composition (fat reduction) is a high-priority goal.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

As with the others, AOD-9604 is typically reconstituted from powder form:

  • Clinician selects a volume of bacteriostatic water for the vial.
  • Concentration is documented in the patient’s instructions.
  • Daily dose is expressed both in micrograms and in mL or “units” on the syringe.

The patient follows a clear, written plan rather than trying to calculate this alone.

IGF-1 LR3 / IGF-1 DES
Advanced muscle tools
Advanced Only

IGF-1 analogues such as LR3 and DES are advanced tools that are sometimes discussed for muscle growth and nutrient partitioning. Their use is generally limited to highly supervised scenarios.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • IGF-1 LR3: typical range discussed is 20–40 mcg pre-workout.
  • IGF-1 DES: often mentioned around 20 mcg in a targeted area pre-workout.
  • Only considered for experienced users under close medical oversight.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Support for lean mass development and nutrient utilization.
  • Potential for site-specific focus (DES) in advanced, medically directed protocols.
Why They’re in SSP-01 (Optional)
  • Reserved for high-level performance contexts with strong medical supervision.
  • Included as an advanced module for individuals whose primary goal is muscle density and strength.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

IGF-1 analogues are strictly advanced-use compounds. In a clinical setting:

  • The clinician handles all reconstitution and concentration decisions.
  • Only precise, written dosing instructions should be followed.
  • Patients should never attempt to improvise doses or mixing volumes.

If IGF-1 is mentioned in your plan, ensure all questions on risk, benefit, and dosing are answered by your medical provider.

Protocol Overview — RGN-02

The Wolverine Regeneration Program (RGN-02) prioritizes healing and connective tissue support. Where SSP-01 leans into performance and body composition, RGN-02 is far more focused on recovery, joint comfort, and structural resilience.

Component Peptide Primary Goal
Local Repair BPC-157 Targeted support for tendons, ligaments, and soft-tissue areas.
Systemic Repair TB-500 System-wide support for healing and mobility.
Remodeling & Collagen GHK-Cu Collagen structure, tissue quality, and long-term resilience.

Daily & Weekly Flow — RGN-02

Again, this is a structural example to show how components could be scheduled. Real treatment plans must be tailored and supervised by a licensed clinician.

Daily

BPC-157 & GHK-Cu

  • BPC-157 used once or twice daily as directed, often associated with the area of concern.
  • GHK-Cu taken daily to support collagen and tissue quality.
Weekly

TB-500 Rhythm

  • TB-500 split into 2–3 doses per week as part of a multi-week recovery cycle.

Peptide Breakdown — RGN-02

Here is the same level of breakdown for Wolverine as we used for Super Soldier — focusing on what each peptide does, why it’s here, and what reconstitution typically means in a clinical environment.

BPC-157
Local repair support
Core Regen

BPC-157 is central to RGN-02 — it is frequently discussed for its potential benefits around tendon, ligament, and localized soft-tissue support.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Commonly discussed total: 250–500 mcg per day.
  • Sometimes split into morning and evening doses.
  • Direction about “local vs systemic” use comes from a clinician.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Supports recovery in tendons and ligaments.
  • Often used along with physical therapy and rehab exercises.
  • May support joint comfort and return to movement.
Why It’s in RGN-02
  • Acts as the “front-line” peptide for targeted tissue comfort and support.
  • Designed to pair with progressive rehab and controlled training.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

For BPC-157 in RGN-02:

  • A clinician selects the vial strength and total planned daily amount.
  • The powder is reconstituted with sterile/bacteriostatic water.
  • The patient is given a simple instruction: “draw up X volume for Y micrograms.”

If anything about those instructions is unclear, patients should ask their provider to walk them through it step-by-step.

TB-500
Systemic healing
Core Regen

TB-500 is designed to work more globally, supporting healing and mobility throughout the body rather than just one targeted area.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Range frequently discussed: 2–5 mg per week.
  • Administered as split doses during the week.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Systemic support for recovery from widespread or multi-site stress.
  • Complements the more local action of BPC-157.
  • Helps support active individuals with multiple stressed joints/tissues.
Why It’s in RGN-02
  • Wolverine isn’t just about one joint; it’s about the whole system.
  • TB-500 is included to “widen the net” of recovery support across the body.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

TB-500 vials for RGN-02 are typically reconstituted similarly to SSP-01:

  • The vial contains a set amount of TB-500 in mg.
  • A clinician chooses how much sterile water to add for a convenient weekly dose.
  • The weekly plan (and each split dose) is laid out clearly in mL or syringe “units.”

All calculations and adjustments should be left to the medical team, not the patient.

GHK-Cu
Collagen & remodeling
Structural Support

In Wolverine, GHK-Cu is geared less toward aesthetics and more toward the quality of healing and collagen structure in the recovering tissues.

Educational Dose & Timing
  • Range often discussed: 1–2 mg per day.
  • Can be timed to overlap with evening recovery, depending on the plan.
Key Benefits (Educational)
  • Supports collagen matrix formation.
  • May improve tissue quality as healing progresses.
  • Adds a structural “finishing layer” to the recovery process.
Why It’s in RGN-02
  • Recovering tissue is not just about speed; it’s about quality.
  • GHK-Cu is included so the collagen framework being laid down can be as robust as possible.
Reconstitution (Educational Overview)

For GHK-Cu in a regeneration-focused protocol:

  • The clinician chooses a vial size and final target daily dose.
  • Mixing is done with sterile fluid to reach a usable concentration.
  • The patient receives simple, clear instructions on daily volume.

Because GHK-Cu can be used in different forms (topical vs injectable), form and dose are strictly provider-dependent.
